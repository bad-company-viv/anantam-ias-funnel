- Personal, conversational medium
- Students check WhatsApp multiple times daily
- Easy to share study materials, PDFs, videos
- Community building through groups

## WhatsApp Group Strategy

### Community Group (All Leads)

**Purpose:** Nurture leads, build community, provide value

**Content Calendar:**

**Daily:**
- Morning: Current affairs summary (7 AM)
- Evening: Motivational quote + study tip (8 PM)

**Weekly:**
- Monday: Week's study plan
- Wednesday: Answer writing practice question
- Friday: Weekly current affairs quiz
- Sunday: Success story / Ranker interview

**Rules:**
- No spam or promotions
- Respect all members
- Only admins can send messages (broadcast group)
- Questions via DM to admin

### Batch Group (Enrolled Students Only)

**Purpose:** Student support, doubt clearing, announcements

**Content:**
- Daily timetable reminders
- Class links and recordings
- Test announcements
- Doubt clearing sessions
- Peer discussions (allowed)
- Faculty announcements

**Rules:**
- Respectful communication
- No off-topic messages
- Doubts in designated time slots
- No spam or external links

## Best Practices

### Timing
- Morning messages: 7-9 AM (current affairs)
- Evening messages: 7-9 PM (motivation, tips)
- Avoid: Late night (after 10 PM)
- Avoid: Early morning (before 7 AM)

### Frequency
- Max 2 messages per day for leads
- Max 3 messages per day for students
- Space messages at least 4 hours apart
- Don't spam - quality over quantity

### Personalization
- Always use first name
- Reference their actions (attended webinar, downloaded PDF)
- Segment based on behavior
- Tailor messages to their stage in funnel

### Compliance
- Always provide opt-out option
- Respect "STOP" requests immediately
- Don't share user data
- Follow WhatsApp Business Policy

### Response Management
- Respond within 5 minutes during business hours
- Use quick replies for common questions
- Escalate complex queries to counselors
- Track all conversations in CRM

## WhatsApp vs Email: When to Use What

### Use WhatsApp For:
- Urgent reminders (webinar starting soon)
- Quick updates (test links, class links)
- Personal check-ins
- Community building
- High-priority announcements

### Use Email For:
- Detailed information (course curriculum)
- Long-form content (study guides)
- Formal communications (enrollment confirmation)
- Resource delivery (PDFs, videos)
- Legal/policy documents

### Use Both For:
- Webinar reminders (redundancy ensures delivery)
- Enrollment follow-ups (multi-channel approach)
- Important deadlines (maximize reach)
- Special offers (increase visibility)
